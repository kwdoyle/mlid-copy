Stress: The Breakfast of Anti-Champions

September 30, 2010

 
 

I’m feeling so very stressed out lately. Nothing seems to be cohesive right now. Mainly, I’m troubled by the fact that 3/4th of the classes I’m taking this semester are essentially pointless for when I change to Biology and how I must excel in them, despite having no real motivation to do so, or else have my GPA forever desecrated to an horrible state of practically no return. It certainly doesn’t help that one of these classes, economics no less, has been annoying me in the sense that I somehow keep doing things wrong even though I am doing what I thought I learned in class. And now our first exam is coming up soon, so I shall soon see how doomed I am with respect to needing to begrudgingly struggle through this basically worthless semester.


This physics class I’m in is yet another class that could be potentially rubbing me the wrong way. For the most part, it eh. (KC inserted that “eh,” which she insisted I keep, and I will because it does express my feelings towards the class). But no, it could maybe count for a basic physics class later on, but I don’t know that for sure. I guess if you want to take a positive approach regarding this all, this physics class is surely helping me realize that I really am not a fan of applied sciences. Y’know, the ones with math. I know even more now that I enjoy life sciences (e.g., Biology) much, much better.

I can’t get around to finally changing my major fast enough…

This weekend. It must happen.


I was also informed that you can, in fact, repair your GPA eventually, but unfortunately, that’s not how my mind works: I can’t do bad academically. I feel like an utter failure if I do, even if it doesn’t affect my grade in the long run. That mark of failure will always be there; omnipresent, if you will, on my transcript. And then I’ll just feel very bad because of that, possibly losing sleep at night and having to cry myself to sleep.


10/1/10 Update:

I guess another thing bothering me is that I feel… lonely. Not in the sense that I don’t acknowledge my friends-- because I do. You are all awesome. (And if you’re not my friend, then you’re still awesome because you’re reading this and I don’t even know who you are). It’s just that I’ve generally always had someone in my life that I regarded as an individual who I cared about in a different way than anyone else. Once that would end with someone, due to a various assortment of reasons, I’d be able to get over it fairly quickly and be fine with it until I found someone else. But… that isn’t happening this time. It concerns me and, quite frankly, is a bit frustrating.
